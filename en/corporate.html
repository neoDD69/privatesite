A straightforward premise…

All companies are made of people and thus, at different stages of their evolution, most companies encounter the same problems. The issues most commonly felt by people at all levels within a company include: communication problems, especially between different departments, accompanied by dislikes and conflicts; low engagement/sense of belonging and poor motivation; and a low propensity for "genuine collaboration." In short, phrases like "The right hand doesn't know what the left hand is doing," "Everyone just looks out for their own backyard," "They make us do pointless things..." are widespread, and what is striking is that these sentiments are shared both at the managerial level and the operational level.

Currently, four distinct generations are coexisting within companies (Baby Boomers, Generation X, Millennials, Generation Z), making it challenging to create a work environment where everyone feels comfortable and can work together. Understanding what people of different ages think and want is crucial for the company to progress and achieve its goals.

Therefore, companies have always been engaged in preventing and addressing these related issues. There are generally three approaches, which are compatible with each other, to generate the right culture, apply best practices, and build a serene working environment:

  1. Consulting: Of various origins and natures, consulting helps define processes, timelines, and methods to operate more efficiently and effectively. This approach works, but it is not always positively received by those working within the company who may feel forced into actions not aligned with their preferences. Experience teaches us that people often seek shortcuts, thinking, "It's written this way, but we do it this other way because it's quicker, it's better..."
  2. Training: On every kind of topic and nuance, training is always important because it inspires and allows for growth by acquiring new tools. It has a positive impact on the company, but the percentage of content that transitions from theory to practice is unfortunately always very low. Therefore, it's necessary to patiently invest many hours of people's time to see results.
    A variant of training is Team Building—an experience always of great value, but then the adventure on the boat, in the mountains, or in the kitchen ends, and people return to daily reality often with the same dynamics as before!
  3. Coaching: Very trendy in the last decade, coaching has an excellent impact because it aims to empower the individual (or small team) by guiding them to find their answers, thereby developing their way of thinking and reflecting on relationships with others and the impact of their actions. However, without a coach, all this cannot be successfully realized, which is why coaching skills are increasingly demanded of managers and are highly popular on resumes. Additionally, empowering the individual is always wonderful, but when that individual leaves the organization, it is necessary to start over from scratch!
